## Alternative Material: EMT Conduit (2026)

EMT (Electrical Metallic Tube) is galvanized steel conduit — much stronger and heavier than PVC. Requires bolted connections (no glue), heavier-duty hubs, and metal-cutting tools.

### EMT Build Adjustments

- **No PVC cement needed** — remove that line item ($22–33 saved)
- **Hubs must be heavier** — 3/4" plywood recommended; add 50% to plywood cost
- **More fasteners required** — EMT needs bolts at every hub; fasteners cost ~1.5× PVC
- **Deeper footings** — extra weight needs more anchoring (rebar + sandbags × 1.5)
- **Assembly time** — add 50% for weight and mechanical connections
- **Tools** — angle grinder with metal cutoff wheels replaces PVC saw
